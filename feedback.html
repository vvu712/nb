Hear from users like YOU...

*surveys performed 2/2 - 2/21

Familiarity and Receptiveness to Acceptance and Commitment Therapy Therapy

Although 87% of respondents were unfamiliar with Acceptance and Commitment therapy, 66% were somewhat receptive to it and 27% were highly receptive to ACT therapy. This highlights the great potentional ACT therapy have on helping users stay motivated to eat healthier.

Noticing the lack of knowledge about the therapy, we expanded upon the page to provide clarification on how our APP would apply ACT.

Current Usage of Food Logging vs Usage of Food Logging with

Due to forgetfulness and a lack of motivation and many other reasons, only 12.2% of respondents log their food data more than serveral times a week. Despite the low number of people actively logging their food data. The survey showed that with an AI bot to assist with this mindless and tedious task, 63.7% would be more likely to log food.

To ease the use of NutriWise Bot, an image recognition aspect was added for efficiency to promote user engagement.

91.9% of participants would recommend NutriWise Bot to others.

Hear from experts in the nutrition field

*surveys performed 2/26 - 2/29

Expansion of AI Chatbot Prototype

In response to valuable input from nutrition experts regarding the importance of providing users with both nutritional and culturally accurate information, as well as user requests for a demonstration of our chatbot's capabilities, we created a working prototype. Our working prototype was made to demonstrate the conversational flow and diverse topics that our chatbot can navigate. Due to the limitations of the software, user information and chats could not be saved.

TRY A DEMO

2 out 4 nutritions when asked about their concerns informed us to be accurate and one suggested information peer-reviewed by dietians and considering cultural traditions, which we plan to incorporate. This prototype is trained on accurate information and is soon to be vetted by nutrition experts, which all of experts surveyed were interested in doing, to ensure its reliability to encourage cultural diversity.

Expansion of Games

With our expert interviews, 4 out of 4 nutrition experts believed in the integration of games to engage users. Initially met with questions about its integration from both users and experts, we've not only addressed concerns but also expanded upon it to exceed expectations.

One of the experts requested for more behavioral support, we've refined the gaming aspect to reward users based on their actions. This dynamic system not only fosters engagement, but also reflects our commitment to evolving alongside user needs.
